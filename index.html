The Player

Behind every legend is a person with excellent posture and a worn-out Shift key. This one is Jeff Franzen.

By daylight Jeff builds maps and data tools. After dark he is WallHackJack — a rogue whose reputation was earned the honest way: flawless positioning, immaculate cooldown discipline, and an uncanny habit of appearing on the other side of geometry that should have stopped him. Guildmates stopped asking how. The name stuck.

Ask him about the Karazhan chess event. Do not ask him about the Deadmines run of ’06. He knows what he did.

Raid Progression

  1. Molten Core

    Vanilla · 2006

    Forty people, one fire lord, zero voice discipline. Jack looted a single Bindings drop and has mentioned it at every guild meeting since.

  2. Karazhan

    The Burning Crusade · 2007

    Solved the chess event by refusing to play it correctly. The opera fight requested he stop stealthing through the curtain.

  3. Icecrown Citadel

    Wrath of the Lich King · 2010

    The Lich King said “no king rules forever.” Jack was already behind him. Nobody saw him move. Standard.

  4. Siege of Orgrimmar

    Mists of Pandaria · 2013

    Garrosh brought an Old God’s heart. Jack brought Shadowstep and a grudge. Only one walked out through the wall.

  5. The Current Tier

    Now

    Cutting edge, as always — in progression and in pathing. The boss room has four walls. Jack counts five exits.
